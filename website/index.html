How It Works

1

Install the Extension

Add VoiceReader to Chrome from the Chrome Web Store or by downloading directly from our website.

2

Click the Floating Icon

While browsing, click the VoiceReader floating icon that appears on any webpage to open the transcription panel.

3

Choose Text Source

Extract all page content, use selected text, or paste your own text into the transcription panel.

4

Customize & Listen

Adjust voice settings to your preference and click "Play Audio" to start listening to the content.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is VoiceReader free to use?

Yes, VoiceReader is completely free. We're committed to making web content more accessible without any cost to users.

Does VoiceReader work offline?

Yes, the basic functionality works offline as it uses your browser's built-in speech synthesis capabilities. However, some voices may require an internet connection.

Can I use VoiceReader on any website?

Yes, VoiceReader works on most websites, though some highly dynamic or secured sites may have limitations.

Does VoiceReader collect my data?

No, VoiceReader does not collect or transmit any user data. All text processing happens locally in your browser.

Will there be support for other browsers?

We're currently working on versions for Firefox, Edge, and Safari. Stay tuned for updates!
